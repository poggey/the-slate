Four ways to open the film.

The current hero stays until you say otherwise. These are the four directions I'd actually defend — each one code-drawn (no photograph to license or generate), each one alive, and each one tied to the story rather than decoration. Scroll, watch each demo, and green-light / kill them by number.

CONCEPT 01

The beam, drawn live

The exact composition you already like — but the photograph is replaced by a code-drawn projector beam: volumetric light, drifting dust, a breath of flicker. It answers to the cursor — the beam leans a degree or two as you move, like someone shifted in the projection booth. Infinitely crisp at any screen size, ~2KB instead of an image.

WHY IT WINS — judges notice generated-not-sourced. The subtle interactivity (light follows you) is the "missing movement" without adding a single element to the composition.

CONCEPT 02

Dust becomes the title

The beam's dust motes drift idly — then once, on load, they gather into THE SLATE, hold for a beat, and release back into drift. The particles never leave; they're the same grammar as the 65% number and the final-frame dots. The site's thesis in one gesture: chaos, briefly organised, then chaos again.

WHY IT WINS — a single memorable beat ("did you see the dust spell the title?") that visitors describe to other people. That's the shareable moment criterion, on the first frame.

CONCEPT 03

The countdown leader

A true cold open: the Academy leader — thin sweep arc, serif numeral, grain — counts 5 · 4 · 3 · 2, then hard-cuts to the title frame. Three and a half seconds, once per session, any scroll or click skips it. The most cinematic possible first impression, and the timecode ticker picks up exactly where the leader leaves off.

WHY IT WINS — restraint plus theatre. Nobody else opens a data story with a projection leader; it commits fully to the conceit and costs the visitor almost nothing.

CONCEPT 04

The marquee ledger

No image at all: the hero is the slate itself — all 58 films rolling as quiet micro-type columns, like end credits played before the film. The five that paid for everything glow gold as they pass. The title sits still over the motion. Typography as image; the dataset as texture.
